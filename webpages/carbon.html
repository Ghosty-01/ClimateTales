What is carbon Dioxide?
 Carbon dioxide is one of the primary greenhouses gases on the earth, which traps heats close to earth. It helps earth hold some of the heat it receives from the sun so it doesn't all escape back into space. But CO2 is only good up to a point beyond that point, earth's temperature warms up too much. Human activities, such as burning fossil fuels and cutting down forest and changing the balance between how much carbon is in the air and how much carbon is stored in plants and ocean. It is the main gas contributing to climate change. Human activities hav raised the atmosphere's carbon dioxide content by 50% in less than 2000.

What is Sources of carbon dioxide?
 Carbon Dioxide comes from both CO2 comes from both natural and human sources. Natural sources include the respiration and decomposition of organisms, weathering of carbonate rocks, forest fires, volcanic eruptions, and the release of CO2 from the ocean. Human sourcesof CO2 emissions come from activities like cement production, deforestation, and the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas.

Impacts of carbon dioxide
 Carbon dioxide has several impacts on the environment, human health, and the economy. Some of the most significant effects include:
Environmental impacts such as global warming and climate change, Ocean Acidification, extreme weather event;
Human Health Impacts such as Respiratory problems, heat-related illness, mental health.

Causes of carbon dioxide
 Carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions primarily result from human activities, significantly contributing to climate change. major causes are Fossil Fuel Combustion, Industrial Processes, Deforestation and Land Use Change, Agriculture, Waste Management, Heating, Natural Processes and Energy Inefficiency
