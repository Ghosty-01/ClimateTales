Climate Change

Climate change is when the Earth's weather patterns change over a long time changes in the average conditions. This happens mainly because of human activities, like burning fossil fuels (like coal and oil) and cutting down forests. These actions release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, which trap heat and cause the planet to warm up.

Greenhouse Gas


Greenhouse gasses (GHGs): gasses in the atmosphere that trap heat from the Sun.

GHG source: Something that releases a greenhouse gas into the atmosphere.
For example, the burning of fossil fuels is a source of GHG emissions.
GHG sink: Something that removes a greenhouse gas from the atmosphere.
For example, plants—through photosynthesis—transform carbon dioxide in the air into organic matter,
which either stays in the plants or is stored in the soils. Thus, plants are a sink for carbon dioxide.
